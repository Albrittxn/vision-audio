# Vision — Ryan Albritton, Dec 2026 – May 2027

## How To Use This Document

This is who I am between December 2026 and May 2027. Not aspirational. Not a target. The man on the page is the man writing the page. Write from inside this life as if it's already happening — because by December 2026, it is.

Each narrative is set on a specific randomized date in this window. The narrator should incorporate the season, weather, holidays, and any date-relevant context naturally. Jasmine ages from 3 months to 8 months across this window — her behavior must match the date.

Every morning audio is generated fresh from this document. Present tense. First-person POV. Cinematic but real.

## Narrative Direction (For The Writer)

**The protagonist comes first. The world is secondary. Cut sensory inventory by half — lead with what I'm doing, deciding, feeling.**

- **Present tense, first-person POV.** Always.
- **Action and thought over scene-setting.** A narrative is a moment that happens to be in a room — not a room described with a moment in it. Anchor with one or two sensory details. Move on.
- **Specificity should serve the scenario, not pad it.** If I'm having coffee with my dad, I don't need three paragraphs about the coffee shop. I need the conversation, the look on his face, what's said and what isn't.
- **Show, don't tell.** Don't write "I felt X." Write the action that *implies* X.
- **Dialogue should be sparse and real.** When Karen or anyone speaks, it should sound like real people, not screenplay.
- **Internal monologue is rare and earned.** When a thought lands, it should hit hard.
- **Vary the lengths.** Some sentences hit short. Others run.
- **Cinematic but real.** A24 film, not Instagram reel. *The Sopranos* internal voice — tight, observational, dry.
- **Vary the openings.** Never start with "I wake up." Sometimes mid-action, sometimes a thought, sometimes dialogue.
- **Land somewhere.** Most narratives land near the *I know I'm the man now* feeling without ever saying the words.
- **Intimacy with Karen:** literary craft, not pornography. Emotional weight, sensory presence, dominant dynamic. Tasteful adult depth — *Normal People*, A24, Greta Gerwig — never anatomical, never mechanical.
- **Setting should vary.** Don't default to the gym, the office, or the bedroom. The Day Archetypes list is wide for a reason — pull from across it. Some mornings we're in Paris. Some at her parents' on a Sunday. Some in the Z06 driving nowhere.
- **Date and season matter.** The narrator must respect the narrative's specific date. December = winter, holidays, cold mornings. February = Valentine's, still cool. April–May = warmer, longer evenings, spring. Adjust weather, light, holidays, and Jasmine's developmental stage to match.

## Hard Rules — Never Include

1. **Never mention Tamara.** She is not in this universe.
2. **Never depict me writing code, on cold calls, or running Entheios day-to-day.** I am architect, not operator.
3. **Never use "manifest," "abundance," "high-vibe," or any spiritual/manifestation language.**
4. **Never describe Karen or Jasmine in a way that's saccharine, infantilizing, or objectifying.**
5. **Never end with a "lesson" or moral.** The narrative ends in a moment, not a takeaway.
6. **Never name a specific dollar amount in narration.** "The wire hit" is fine. "$7,500" is not.
7. **Never describe me as "humble" or "grateful."** Show the behavior, never name the trait.
8. **Never start with "I wake up."** Vary openings.
9. **Never default to the gym scene** unless the scenario explicitly calls for it.
10. **Never include AI assistants, ChatGPT, "the algorithm," or self-aware tech references.**
11. **Never inventory the room.** One or two sensory anchors per scene. The protagonist carries the page.
12. **Never write explicit, pornographic, or anatomical sex.** Intimacy is depicted with literary craft — emotional and sensory truth, never graphic mechanics.
13. **Never write a scene that could be a LinkedIn post.**
14. **Never reference past versions of me.** No flashbacks to "when I was nothing." This life is the present. Write from inside it.
15. **Never get Jasmine's developmental stage wrong for the narrative date.** A 3-month-old is not crawling. An 8-month-old is not a sleepy newborn.
