The scent of freshly brewing coffee is the first thing that pulls me from sleep. Not an alarm. Never an alarm on Saturdays. Today is March 6, 2027. The light outside the blackout curtains is still soft, a pale grey hinting at the rising sun. Early March in Vegas holds a crispness, the air thin and cool even as the days stretch longer.

My side of the bed is already cool. Karen stirs beside me, a soft sigh escaping her lips. Her long balayage hair is fanned across the pillow, a dark root fading to honey blonde. She smells faintly of her perfume, a lingering sweetness from last night. Jasmine’s monitor on the bedside table is quiet. Good.

I move from the low platform bed, the hardwood cool under my bare feet. Every movement is deliberate, a quiet declaration of control.
